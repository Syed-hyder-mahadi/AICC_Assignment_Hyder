Description

Customer segmentation is a technique used to divide customers into different groups based on similar characteristics such as income and spending habits. In this assignment, we use K-Means clustering to analyze a mall dataset and identify different customer groups.

Companies such as Amazon and Walmart use similar techniques to understand customer behavior and improve marketing strategies.

Python Code (K-Means Clustering)
import pandas as pd
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load the dataset
data = pd.read_csv("Mall_Customers.csv")

# Select relevant features
X = data[['Annual Income (k$)', 'Spending Score (1-100)']]

# Apply K-Means clustering
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=5, random_state=42)
data['Cluster'] = kmeans.fit_predict(X)

# Visualize clusters
plt.scatter(X['Annual Income (k$)'], X['Spending Score (1-100)'], c=data['Cluster'])
plt.xlabel("Annual Income (k$)")
plt.ylabel("Spending Score (1-100)")
plt.title("Customer Segmentation using K-Means")
plt.show()
Customer Groups Identified

After applying K-Means clustering, the following customer groups can be identified:

High Income – High Spending
These customers earn a lot and spend frequently. They are the most valuable customers for the mall.
High Income – Low Spending
Customers with high income but low spending habits. Businesses may target them with special offers.
Low Income – High Spending
These customers spend more despite having lower income.
Low Income – Low Spending
Customers who spend less and may not contribute significantly to revenue.
Average Income – Moderate Spending
Regular customers with balanced spending patterns.
Conclusion

Customer segmentation helps businesses understand different types of customers and design better marketing strategies. Using machine learning algorithms like K-Means clustering, companies can analyze data patterns and improve customer engagement and sales.
