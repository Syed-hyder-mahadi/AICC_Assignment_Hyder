Description

Customer segmentation is a technique used in machine learning to divide customers into groups based on similar characteristics. In this assignment, we use K-Means Clustering to analyze a mall dataset and identify different types of customers based on their behavior.

Businesses and online shopping platforms such as Amazon often use similar techniques to understand customer preferences and improve marketing strategies.

Python Code (K-Means Clustering)
import pandas as pd
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load dataset
data = pd.read_csv("Mall_Customers.csv")

# Select features (Annual Income and Spending Score)
X = data[['Annual Income (k$)', 'Spending Score (1-100)']]

# Apply K-Means clustering
kmeans = KMeans(n_clusters=5, random_state=0)
data['Cluster'] = kmeans.fit_predict(X)

# Plot clusters
plt.scatter(X['Annual Income (k$)'], X['Spending Score (1-100)'], c=data['Cluster'])
plt.xlabel("Annual Income (k$)")
plt.ylabel("Spending Score")
plt.title("Customer Segmentation using K-Means")
plt.show()
Customer Groups Identified

After applying K-Means clustering, customers can be divided into several groups:

High Income – High Spending
Customers who earn a lot and spend frequently. These are premium customers.
High Income – Low Spending
Customers with high income but low spending. They may need targeted marketing.
Low Income – High Spending
Customers who spend more despite lower income.
Low Income – Low Spending
Customers who spend less and may not be highly engaged.
Average Income – Moderate Spending
Regular customers with balanced income and spending patterns.
Conclusion

Customer segmentation helps businesses understand different types of customers and create better marketing strategies. Machine learning techniques like K-Means clustering can reveal useful patterns in data and improve business decisions.
